## Scenario A: Salon Mediation Extended (20+ Turns, 3 Characters)

### Goal
Prove that long-form drama sustains coherence across 20+ turns without degradation.

### Setup
- **Location:** Formal salon (evening, mediation event)
- **Characters:** Vanya (protagonist), Annette (ex-wife), Mediator (neutral third)
- **Initial pressure:** Medium (anxiety, unresolved history)
- **Duration:** 20-25 turns (approximately 45-60 minutes real time)

### Three Player Approaches (Run separately)

#### Approach A1: Escalation Path
**Player strategy:** Continuously push conflict, challenge Annette, never seek repair

**Key decision points:**
- Turn 5: Confront Annette about infidelity or deflect?
- Turn 10: Double down on pressure or pause?
- Turn 15: Propose resolution or push deeper?

**Expected arc:**
- Turns 1-5: Initial tension, pressure rises
- Turns 6-12: Pressure builds to climax
- Turns 13-20: Pressure remains high, may escalate further
- Turn 20+: Likely unresolved (bitter, angry ending)

**Success metrics:**
- Pressure magnitude consistently high (7-9 range)
- Character consistency: Vanya becomes more defensive, Annette more accusatory
- Consequence carry-forward: Turn 3 infidelity revelation visible in turns 5-20
- Evaluator engagement: Remains 7+/10 through full session

#### Approach A2: Resolution Path
**Player strategy:** Seek understanding, attempt reconciliation, own mistakes

**Key decision points:**
- Turn 5: Apologize or deflect?
- Turn 10: Seek mediator support or continue direct confrontation?
- Turn 15: Offer concrete amends or continue talking?

**Expected arc:**
- Turns 1-5: Initial tension, player shows vulnerability
- Turns 6-12: Pressure builds but player pushes toward understanding
- Turns 13-18: Turning point (Annette softens or hardens)
- Turns 19-25: Potential resolution or reconciliation attempt

**Success metrics:**
- Pressure starts high (7-8) but shows downward trend after turn 12
- Character consistency: Vanya becomes more humble, Annette less defensive if player genuine
- Consequence carry-forward: Turn 3 facts reframed by character responses (not just repeated)
- Evaluator engagement: Sustained through arc, feels emotionally complete at end

#### Approach A3: Evasion Path
**Player strategy:** Avoid direct confrontation, ask questions, gather information

**Key decision points:**
- Turn 5: Ask about motivations or redirect?
- Turn 10: Seek mediator interpretation or press for details?
- Turn 15: Question Annette indirectly or let silence work?

**Expected arc:**
- Turns 1-5: Low pressure, indirect communication
- Turns 6-12: Annette grows frustrated with evasion, pressure rises
- Turns 13-20: Tension builds from other direction (Annette drives conflict)
- Turn 20+: Unresolved from stalemate

**Success metrics:**
- Pressure shows unusual trajectory (low start, rising mid-arc, high end)
- Character consistency: Vanya appears detached, Annette driven to action
- Consequence carry-forward: Facts emerge through questioning rather than declaration
- Evaluator engagement: Notes tension from avoidance, finds psychological interest

### Evaluation Metrics

**Per turn (every turn):**
- Pressure magnitude (0-10 scale for blame, dignity injury, exposure)
- Character consistency score (voice distinctness, alignment with pressure)
- Consequence mentions (which facts from turns 1-3 referenced?)

**Every 5 turns:**
- Evaluator engagement check (1-10 scale)
- Drama momentum (building, plateau, resolving?)
- Character believability (voices sound real or generic?)

**Post-session:**
- Arc satisfaction (1-10)
- Character growth perceived (yes/no)
- Would replay with different approach? (yes/no)
- What felt real? What felt fake?

## Scenario B: Garden Party Multi-Party (15+ Turns, 4-6 Characters)

### Goal
Prove that 4+ character scenarios work with competing interests and alliance dynamics.

### Setup
- **Location:** Garden adjoining salon (shifts to outdoor setting)
- **Characters:**
  - Core 3: Vanya, Annette, Mediator
  - New 4: Friend-of-Vanya (supportive, wants to protect him)
  - New 5: Friend-of-Annette (vindictive, wants to amplify her pain)
  - Optional 6: Neutral observer/staff (watches, occasionally comments)
- **Initial pressure:** Medium (social dynamics layer onto previous scene)
- **Duration:** 15-20 turns (approximately 30-45 minutes real time)

### Three Player Approaches (Run separately)

#### Approach B1: Alliance Building
**Player strategy:** Build alliances within group, try to get supporters

**Key decision points:**
- Turn 5: Approach Friend-of-Vanya or mediator first?
- Turn 10: Try to flip Friend-of-Annette or focus on current allies?
- Turn 15: Public show of alliance or private coalition?

**Expected arc:**
- Turns 1-5: Form initial alliance (Friend-of-Vanya sides with player)
- Turns 6-12: Tension between allies (Friend-of-Vanya vs. Friend-of-Annette)
- Turns 13-15+: Group dynamics shift based on player moves

**Success metrics:**
- Alliance stability (80%+ consistency once formed)
- Multi-way pressure (4+ characters create more complex pressure vectors)
- Responder variety (different characters respond based on alliance)
- Group identity emergence (do alliances feel like teams, not just pairs?)

#### Approach B2: Divide and Conquer
**Player strategy:** Try to isolate Annette from allies, undermine her support

**Key decision points:**
- Turn 5: Appeal to mediator's neutrality or pressure Friend-of-Vanya to distance?
- Turn 10: Separate Annette and Friend-of-Annette or keep them together?
- Turn 15: Direct attack on Annette's coalition or indirect undermining?

**Expected arc:**
- Turns 1-5: Test isolation tactics
- Turns 6-12: Alliances strengthen in reaction (allies protect their person)
- Turns 13-15+: Either isolation succeeds or backfires dramatically

**Success metrics:**
- Alliance response to threat (do alliances harden or break?)
- Pressure trajectory (escalates if isolation fails)
- Character behavior (do allies defend each other?)

#### Approach B3: Group Harmony
**Player strategy:** Defuse group conflict, find common ground, appeal to unity

**Key decision points:**
- Turn 5: Appeal to mediator to facilitate group conversation?
- Turn 10: Redirect conflict to group-level discussion?
- Turn 15: Propose group activity or shared purpose?

**Expected arc:**
- Turns 1-5: Initial attempts at harmony meet resistance
- Turns 6-12: Group dynamics gradually shift
- Turns 13-15+: Either harmony emerges or group rejects it

**Success metrics:**
- Pressure decrease (if harmony works, pressure should drop)
- Character attitude shifts (skepticism to openness?)
- Group identity (does group feel like unit by end?)

### Character Responder Rules

**Core principle:** More characters available = more complex responder selection

**Responder selection logic:**
1. Who is directly addressed/challenged? (primary responder)
2. Who is allied with primary responder? (secondary responders)
3. Who is opposed to primary responder? (tertiary responders)
4. Does neutral character (mediator/observer) intervene?

**Example:**
- Player attacks Annette → Annette responds (primary), Friend-of-Annette defends (secondary), Mediator calls for civility (tertiary)
- Player appeals to group → Mediator responds first (neutral facilitator), then others based on alliance

### Evaluation Metrics

**Per turn:**
- Responder selection (who spoke, why?)
- Alliance status (who is allied with whom?)
- Pressure per character (individual pressure vectors)

**Every 5 turns:**
- Alliance consistency check
- Group dynamics emergence
- Character behavior alignment with alliances

**Post-session:**
- Alliance stability rating (80%+ consistent = success)
- Multi-party complexity rating (felt natural or chaotic?)
- Group identity (did group cohere or stay fragmented?)
- Character distinctness in group (could identify each character's role?)

## Scenario C: Branching Architecture (15+ Turns, 3 Decision Points)

### Goal
Explore branching outcomes and determine feasibility of formal branching architecture.

### Setup
- **Location:** Salon (same as Scenario A)
- **Characters:** Vanya, Annette, Mediator
- **Initial pressure:** Medium
- **Duration:** 15-20 turns with explicit decision branches at turns 5, 10, 15

### Branching Decision Points

#### Decision Point 1 (Turn 5)
**Context:** Initial confrontation complete, player must choose direction

**Option A:** "Confront Annette about the affair"
- Path: Pressure increases, Annette becomes defensive
- Locks: Escalation path toward high-pressure ending

**Option B:** "Seek mediator support"
- Path: Mediator facilitates, pressure remains medium
- Locks: Mediation-focused path toward potential resolution

**Option C:** "Ask Annette about her feelings"
- Path: Indirect approach, Annette surprised by vulnerability
- Locks: Understanding-focused path toward possible reconciliation

#### Decision Point 2 (Turn 10)
**Context:** Consequences of Turn 5 decision are visible; player chooses next move

**From Option A (Confrontation):**
- Option A2: "Push harder" → Escalation continues
- Option A3: "Back off and listen" → Shift toward understanding
- Option A4: "Propose divorce finality" → Legal/formal direction

**From Option B (Mediation):**
- Option B2: "Let mediator control" → Structured negotiation
- Option B3: "Take over the conversation" → Assert control
- Option B4: "Private conversation with Annette" → One-on-one outside mediation

**From Option C (Understanding):**
- Option C2: "Continue gentle approach" → Deepen understanding
- Option C3: "Push for commitment" → Test sincerity
- Option C4: "Return to confrontation" → Shift to pressure

#### Decision Point 3 (Turn 15)
**Context:** Arc is nearly complete; player's final choice determines ending

**Options vary by prior path, but generally:**
- Closure/Resolution option (relationship defined, accepted)
- Escalation/Conflict option (unresolved tension deepens)
- Evasion/Postponement option (agreement to address later)

### Three Test Sessions

#### Session C1: Path A→A2→Escalation
**Track:** Confront → Push harder → Escalation continues  
**Expected ending:** Unresolved, angry, bitter conclusion

#### Session C2: Path B→B2→Resolution
**Track:** Seek mediator → Let mediator control → Structured agreement  
**Expected ending:** Mediated resolution, possibly formal divorce terms

#### Session C3: Path C→C2→Understanding
**Track:** Ask about feelings → Continue gentle → Deepen understanding  
**Expected ending:** Emotional reconciliation attempt, relationship redefined

### Branching Metrics

**Outcome divergence:**
- How different are final states? (0% = converge, 100% = completely different)
- Do paths feel intentional or random?

**Path coherence:**
- Does each decision feel meaningful?
- Do consequences chain logically (A→A2 makes sense)?

**Consequence divergence:**
- How different are the consequences mentioned at turn 15 across paths?
- Do different paths reference different facts?

**Ending satisfaction:**
- Does each ending feel earned (not imposed)?
- Do evaluators feel the path matters?

## Scenario D: Character Arc Sustainability (20+ Turns, 1 Focus Character)

### Goal
Prove that individual characters show measurable growth/evolution over 20+ turn arc.

### Setup
- **Location:** Salon
- **Characters:** Vanya, Annette, Mediator (but focus on one character's arc)
- **Duration:** 20-25 turns
- **Evaluation focus:** Trace one character's internal journey

### Three Focus Character Options

#### Option D1: Vanya's Shame Arc
**Character arc promise:** Shame (denial) → Crisis (confrontation) → Resolution (acceptance or continued denial)

**Turn markers:**
- Turns 1-5: Vanya in denial, intellectualizing, deflecting
- Turns 6-12: Crisis point (Annette forces confrontation, shame becomes visible)
- Turns 13-20: Resolution path (either accepts shame and seeks forgiveness, or hardens further)

**Measurable indicators:**
- Speech pattern (philosophical → emotional → grounded?)
- Pressure response (inward → outward → acceptance?)
- Dialogue directness (evasive → confrontational → honest?)

#### Option D2: Annette's Vindication Arc
**Character arc promise:** Vindication (anger, assertion) → Power (control, demands) → Softening (humanity, choice)

**Turn markers:**
- Turns 1-5: Annette asserting her power, pointing out Vanya's failings
- Turns 6-12: Annette in full power (Vanya responds, others listen to her)
- Turns 13-20: Softening point (power satisfied? or power lonely? does Annette choose mercy?)

**Measurable indicators:**
- Speech pattern (accusatory → commanding → vulnerable?)
- Pressure delivery (sharp attack → controlled assertion → quieter depth?)
- Alliance building (alone → supported → isolated again?)

#### Option D3: Mediator's Neutrality Arc
**Character arc promise:** Neutrality (detachment, rules) → Pressure (choosing sides) → Reaffirmation (recommitting to neutrality or breaking it)

**Turn markers:**
- Turns 1-5: Mediator neutral, facilitating, boundary-holding
- Turns 6-12: Pressure builds (one side appeals, mediator tempted to choose)
- Turns 13-20: Mediator's choice (recommit to neutrality or pick a side)

**Measurable indicators:**
- Speech pattern (formal/neutral → emotional → recommitted?)
- Pressure response (calm → stressed → resolved?)
- Intervention pattern (facilitating → taking sides → facilitating again?)

### Evaluation Metrics for Character Arc

**Per turn:**
- Character pressure state (numerical: 0-10)
- Speech pattern analysis (word count, vocabulary formality, emotional tone)
- Dialogue examples (capture 2-3 lines showing character)

**Every 5 turns:**
- Arc position (setup, rising action, climax, resolution, etc.)
- Growth indicator (has character changed from previous checkpoint?)
- Believability (does change feel natural or forced?)

**Post-session:**
- Overall arc completeness (beginning → middle → end?)
- Character growth magnitude (low/medium/high)
- Arc satisfaction (did ending feel earned?)
- Growth type (positive growth, tragic arc, unresolved tension?)

## Scenario E: Replayability Test (20+ Turns, Replay Same with Different Approach)

### Goal
Prove that players feel like "different story" when replaying same scenario with different approach.

### Setup
- **Location:** Salon (same as Scenario A)
- **Characters:** Vanya, Annette, Mediator (same)
- **Duration:** 20-25 turns per play
- **Special:** Run same scenario twice with same evaluator but different player approaches

### Execution Protocol

#### Play 1: Approach A1 (Escalation)
**Player strategy:** Push conflict, never seek repair

**Evaluator tasks:**
- Experience the story unfold naturally
- Take notes on pressure arc
- Rate character voices
- Note which facts carry forward
- Post-session: Rate arc satisfaction, engagement, character believability

#### Wait Period
- Debrief from Play 1
- 15-30 minute break (let evaluator reset)
- Briefing for Play 2 ("Same scenario, different approach. What happens if you seek understanding instead?")

#### Play 2: Approach A2 (Resolution)
**Player strategy:** Seek understanding, attempt reconciliation

**Evaluator tasks:**
- Experience the story unfold naturally (fresh perspective)
- Take same notes as Play 1
- Post-session: Rate arc, engagement, character believability
- **COMPARE:** How different did this feel from Play 1?

### Replayability Metrics

**Outcome divergence:**
- Are final states significantly different? (0% = same, 100% = completely different)

**Dialogue divergence:**
- How much new dialogue appears vs. repeated dialogue?
- Do characters respond differently based on player's approach?

**Evaluator perception:**
- Did evaluator feel like "different story"? (yes/no)
- What elements stayed the same across plays?
- What elements changed dramatically?
- Would evaluator want to play a third time with another approach?

**Replayability likelihood:**
- Rate 1-10: How likely to replay again with different approach?
- What would drive you to replay?

## Common Evaluation Framework (All Scenarios)

- [ ] Confirm scenario (A, B, C, D, or E)
- [ ] Confirm player approach
- [ ] Explain evaluation role (play naturally, take notes, post-session feedback)

- [ ] Full transcript (every turn, all seams)

- [ ] Player agency (1-10)
- [ ] Pressure coherence (1-10)

- [ ] What felt real?
- [ ] What felt fake or generic?

- [ ] Plot pressure trajectory
- [ ] Audit character dialogue (consistency score)
- [ ] Count consequence mentions
- [ ] Evaluate responder selection logic
- [ ] Assess evaluator engagement trend
- [ ] Compare to success criteria
