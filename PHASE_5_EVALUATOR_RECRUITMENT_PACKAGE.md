## Evaluator Role Overview

### What You'll Be Doing
You'll play the role of "player" in extended dramatic scenarios (20-25 turns, 45-60 minutes per session). Your moves shape the story; the game responds with consequence and character reactions. Your job is to:

1. **Play naturally** — Make moves that feel authentic to you (don't try to "test" the system)
2. **Take notes** — Every 5 turns, pause and note your observations
3. **Provide feedback** — After each session, complete a structured survey
4. **Reflect on experience** — Tell us what felt real and what felt fake

### Who Should Apply
- **Phase 4 returnees:** If you participated in baseline evaluation, we'd love to have you back (you know the system, can compare extended play to short sessions)
- **New participants:** No prior game knowledge needed; we'll brief you fully
- **Requirements:** English-speaking, 2+ hours available (one 45-60 min session plus brief debrief), comfortable with narrative/dramatic games

### What We're Studying
We want to understand:
1. Can this game sustain drama across 20+ turns? (not just 5-7 turn demos)
2. Can multiple characters work together? (4+ people in same scene)
3. Do your choices lead to different outcomes? (branching paths)
4. Do characters feel real? (voice consistency, believable growth)

## What to Expect: Session Flow

### Before Session (30 min before)
1. **Check-in:** Confirm tech setup (if remote)
2. **Briefing:** 10-15 min overview of scenario
   - Where you are (salon, garden, etc.)
   - Who's present (Vanya, Annette, etc.)
   - What's happening (mediation, party, etc.)
   - Your role: Play naturally, not to "win"
3. **Questions:** Ask anything about the scenario

### During Session (45-60 min)
1. **Play:** You make moves (text input), game responds
2. **Checkpoint every 5 turns:** Pause 1 min to note observations
   - Example: "How is the drama feeling? Is the character voice clear? Are consequences visible?"
3. **Natural breaks:** If you feel like pausing, just say so (we want you comfortable, not rushed)

### After Session (15-30 min)
1. **Debrief conversation** (5-10 min)
   - What surprised you?
   - What felt real? What felt fake?
   - Would you want to play again?
2. **Structured survey** (10-15 min)
   - Quantitative ratings (1-10 scales)
   - Open-ended questions (what worked, what didn't)
   - Character feedback (did they feel distinct, believable?)

## Sample Scenario Brief

### Example: Salon Mediation Extended

**Setting:** You're Vanya, ex-husband trying to mediate your divorce with Annette (your ex-wife) and a professional mediator. You're in a formal salon on an evening when everything should be civilized but emotions are high. You have 20+ turns to navigate this conversation.

**Your role:** You're not playing against the system; you're exploring a dramatic situation. Make moves that feel authentic to you. That might mean escalating conflict, seeking understanding, or avoiding confrontation—whatever feels right in the moment.

**What the game does:** Your moves trigger character responses (Annette reacts, mediator responds), state changes (pressure increases if conflict escalates), and consequences (facts established in early turns echo later).

**Success:** There's no "winning." Success is a meaningful story—whether you achieve reconciliation, escalate to bitter anger, or something in between.

## Evaluator Instructions (In-Session)

### How to Play
1. **Read the scene** — System shows: scene description, characters present, your current state
2. **Make a move** — Type what you want to do (e.g., "Confront Annette about the affair" or "Ask the mediator for advice")
3. **Game responds** — Within a few seconds, you see character dialogue, scene changes, your character's thoughts
4. **Repeat** — Continue naturally until session ends (~20-25 turns)

### Checkpoint Notes (Every 5 Turns)
Stop and jot down:
- **Pressure momentum:** Is tension building, stable, or resolving?
- **Character voices:** Can you tell Vanya, Annette, and Mediator apart from dialogue alone?
- **Consequences:** Do earlier facts (things revealed in turns 1-5) show up again?
- **Your engagement:** Are you interested in what happens next? (1-10 scale)
- **Anything weird?** Did anything feel inconsistent, repetitive, or broken?

These notes take 1-2 minutes; game will pause for you.

### During Session: If Something Breaks
- **Game crashes:** Tell facilitator immediately
- **Move doesn't work:** Tell facilitator, try different phrasing
- **Character seems broken:** Note it in checkpoint, continue playing
- **You want to pause:** Say so; we can pause 5-10 min anytime

## Post-Session Survey (15 minutes)

### Quantitative Ratings (1-10 scale)

1. **Arc satisfaction** — How satisfying did the overall story feel?
2. **Character consistency** — Did each character sound like themselves throughout?
3. **Player agency** — Did your moves feel like they mattered?
4. **Pressure coherence** — Did the dramatic pressure feel believable and motivated?
5. **Consequence visibility** — Could you tell earlier facts were affecting the story?
6. **Engagement level** — How interested were you in what happened next?

### Open-Ended Questions

1. **What felt real?** (give 2-3 examples)
2. **What felt fake or generic?** (give 2-3 examples)
3. **Character observations** — Which character felt most real? Least real?
4. **Replay interest** — Would you want to play this scenario again with a different approach?
5. **Biggest takeaway** — In one sentence, what was the key thing that made this feel like drama?

## Evaluator FAQ

**Q: Do I need to understand the technical system?**  
A: No. You just play. We handle the technical side. If something breaks, tell us.

**Q: Will my choices actually matter, or is it on rails?**  
A: Your choices shape the story. Different approaches (escalation vs. reconciliation vs. evasion) lead to genuinely different outcomes. We're testing if that's true.

**Q: How long is each session really?**  
A: ~45-60 minutes play + 15 min debrief. Most people find it flows faster than clock time because it's engaging.

**Q: What if I don't like the character I'm playing?**  
A: You're not in control of everything (you can't make Vanya happy if the situation is inherently sad), but your approach shapes how Vanya responds. Embrace the emotional journey.

**Q: What if I get stuck mid-session?**  
A: Tell the facilitator. We can help clarify, but we won't guide your moves. Your authentic approach is what we're studying.

**Q: Can I request a specific scenario or approach?**  
A: We assign scenarios to match our testing needs, but we'll do our best to accommodate preferences if possible.

**Q: Will you use my feedback for something?**  
A: Yes. Your observations (what felt real, what felt fake) directly inform improvements. If character voice breaks down at turn 15, we fix it before next evaluator.
