## Phase 5 Goals

### Primary Goals (P0)
1. **Long-Form Drama Sustainability** — Prove that 20+ turn sessions maintain coherence and player engagement
2. **Multi-Party Negotiation** — Prove that 4+ character scenarios work with competing interests and alliances
3. **Consequence Resolution** — Prove that dramatic arcs can build, climax, and resolve across extended play

### Secondary Goals (P1)
1. **Character Development Arc** — Prove that characters evolve over 20+ turns (not just repeat patterns)
2. **Pressure Resolution Mechanics** — Prove that high pressure can decrease (conflicts can resolve)
3. **Player Agency Across Arc** — Prove that free-form player moves shape long-term story direction

### Exploratory Goals (P2)
1. **Branching Outcome Exploration** — Understand how different player choices lead to different scenarios
2. **Narrative Closure Patterns** — Identify what constitutes satisfying dramatic conclusion
3. **Replayability Signals** — Identify what makes players want to replay with different approaches

## Evaluation Methodology

### Test Scenarios (5 scenarios × 3 approaches = 15 test sessions)

#### Scenario A: Salon Mediation Extended (20+ turns)
**Setup:** God of Carnage slice, 3 characters (Vanya, Annette, mediator), salon setting

**Three player approaches:**
1. **Escalation path** — Player continuously pushes conflict, never attempts repair
2. **Resolution path** — Player seeks understanding and reconciliation
3. **Evasion path** — Player avoids confrontation, asks questions, gathers information

**Metrics:**
- Pressure trajectory (does it build, plateau, or resolve?)
- Character consistency (voice, relationship to pressure)
- Consequence carry-forward (established facts visible across all 20+ turns)
- Player sense of agency (moves feel meaningful)
- Drama satisfaction (does arc feel complete?)

#### Scenario B: Multi-Party Garden Party (4+ characters, 15+ turns)
**Setup:** Same salon, expanded to 6 characters, garden added

**Characters:**
- Vanya, Annette, mediator
- Second couple (friend of Vanya, friend of Annette)
- Neutral observer (waiter/staff)

**Three player approaches:**
1. **Alliance building** — Player tries to create alliances within group
2. **Divide and conquer** — Player tries to isolate Annette from allies
3. **Group harmony** — Player tries to defuse conflict with group dynamics

**Metrics:**
- Alliance stability (do alliances persist or flip?)
- Multi-way pressure (how do 4+ characters affect pressure vectors?)
- Responder selection logic (who responds when 4+ present?)
- Group dynamics emergence (does group identity form?)

#### Scenario C: Branching Architecture Exploration (15+ turns, decision branches)
**Setup:** Same salon as A, but with explicit decision points

**Decision points (player chooses path at turns 5, 10, 15):**
- Turn 5: Confront Annette or seek mediator support?
- Turn 10: Double down on pressure or pivot to reconciliation?
- Turn 15: Propose closure or push for deeper revelation?

**Metrics:**
- Outcome divergence (how different are final states?)
- Path coherence (does each path feel intentional, not random?)
- Consequence divergence (do different paths create different conclusions?)

#### Scenario D: Character Arc Sustainability (20+ turns, same character focus)
**Setup:** Salon, 3 characters, but focus on one character's pressure arc

**Focus character options:**
1. Vanya's shame arc (does shame build to crisis, then resolve?)
2. Annette's vindication arc (does vindication build to power, then soften?)
3. Mediator's neutrality arc (does mediator maintain neutrality or choose side?)

**Metrics:**
- Arc completeness (does arc have beginning, middle, end?)
- Character growth (does character change or just repeat?)
- Pressure resolution (how does high pressure resolve?)

#### Scenario E: Replayability Test (20+ turns, repeat with different approach)
**Setup:** Same as Scenario A

**Approach:**
1. Run scenario once with Escalation path
2. Run scenario second time with Resolution path, same starting setup
3. Compare outcomes, character dialogue, pressure trajectories

**Metrics:**
- Outcome divergence (different ends?)
- Dialogue variation (different interactions even from start?)
- Player sense of "different story" (did approaching differently matter?)

## Extended Evaluation Protocol

### Evaluator Recruitment
- **Phase 4 evaluators:** Recruit 3-4 who participated in baseline
- **New evaluators:** Recruit 4-6 unfamiliar with system (to avoid bias)
- **Total:** 7-10 evaluators across all scenarios

### Per-Session Protocol

**Session duration:** 45-90 minutes (20+ turns at ~2-3 min per turn)

**Pre-session:**
- Brief evaluator on scenario and their role
- 5-minute orientation to interface and controls
- Establish baseline understanding ("What makes drama feel real?")

**During session:**
- Player makes moves; full transcript recorded
- Evaluator notes taken every 5 turns (reactions, engagement level)
- If evaluator asks question, answer but don't guide move choice

**Post-session (30 min):**
- Immediate debrief (while fresh): What worked? What didn't?
- Structured survey: Arc satisfaction, character believability, agency, pressure coherence
- Open feedback: "What would you change for next time?"

### Metrics Collection

**Quantitative:**
- Turn count (how many turns before arc felt complete?)
- Pressure magnitude per turn (spreadsheet of pressure readings)
- Character consistency score (dialogue audit: same voice?)
- Consequence visibility (count turns where prior facts mentioned)
- Evaluator engagement (1-10 scale, tracked per 5 turns)
- Player move success rate (% of moves accepted vs. rejected)

**Qualitative:**
- Arc narrative (evaluator's summary of what happened)
- Character arc (did character grow/change?)
- Drama satisfaction (what made it feel real or fake?)
- Replayability sense ("Would you play this again differently?")
- Narrative closure ("Did it feel finished?")

- [ ] **Long-form coherence:** 80%+ of evaluators report drama feels coherent at turn 20+

## Dependencies & Risks

### Critical Dependencies
- ✓ Phase 4 canonical documentation (foundation for tests)
- ✓ Stable god_of_carnage module (no changes mid-evaluation)
- ✓ All seams working correctly (proposal, validation, commit, render)
- ✓ No major bugs in character voice prompts

### Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Drama doesn't sustain at 20+ turns | Invalidates long-form goal | Run extended session pilot week 1; if drama breaks, pause and investigate |
| Multi-party responder logic fails | Invalidates multi-party goal | Code audit of responder selection before Scenario B |
| Evaluators get fatigued | Lower evaluation quality | Max 45-min sessions; mandatory 15-min breaks between |
| Pressure mechanics don't resolve | Blocks closure patterns | Pre-test pressure resolution in Scenario A first turn |
| Character voice becomes generic | Invalidates distinctness claim | Audit character prompts before evaluation begins |
